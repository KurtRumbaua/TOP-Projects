Beef Wellington

Beef Wellington is an exquisite and elegant dish that features a tender beef fillet coated with a rich layer of pâté, often made from mushrooms, and then wrapped in a flaky puff pastry. This classic dish is typically seasoned with herbs and spices, providing a depth of flavor that complements the beef beautifully. When baked, the pastry turns golden brown and crispy, encasing the succulent meat inside, making it a stunning centerpiece for special occasions and festive dinners. Traditionally served with a variety of sides such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a rich red wine sauce, Beef Wellington is renowned for its combination of textures and flavors. The preparation involves several steps, including searing the beef, preparing the mushroom duxelles, and carefully wrapping everything in pastry. While it requires some culinary skill, the impressive presentation and delightful taste make it a rewarding dish for both novice and experienced cooks alike.

Ingredients

For the Beef Wellington:
  • Beef Fillet (about 2 pounds, center-cut)
  • Pâté (e.g., foie gras or mushroom pâté)
  • Mushrooms (8 oz, finely chopped)
  • Shallots (2, minced)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
  • Fresh Thyme (1-2 teaspoons, chopped)
  • Dijon Mustard (2 tablespoons)
  • Puff Pastry (about 1 pound, thawed if frozen)
  • Egg (1, beaten for egg wash)
  • Salt and Pepper (to taste)
For the Sides (Optional):
  • Vegetables (such as carrots, asparagus, or green beans)
  • Mashed Potatoes or Roasted Potatoes
  • Red Wine Sauce (for drizzling, made from red wine and beef stock)
